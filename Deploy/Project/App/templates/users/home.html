What is Liver Disease?

Liver disease refers to any condition that impairs liver function. The liver is essential for various bodily functions, including detoxifying harmful substances, producing bile, and regulating metabolism. Liver disease can range from mild to severe and may be acute or chronic.

  • Common causes include viral infections, excessive alcohol consumption, and certain medications.
  • Symptoms may include jaundice, abdominal pain, and fatigue.
  • Effective management often involves lifestyle changes, medication, and in some cases, surgery.

Liver disease can be detected through blood tests, imaging studies, and sometimes liver biopsy. Early detection and treatment are crucial to managing liver health and preventing severe complications. If you experience symptoms or have risk factors, consult a healthcare provider for appropriate evaluation and care.

What is Fatty Liver Disease?

Introduction to Fatty Liver Disease

Fatty liver disease, also known as hepatic steatosis, is a condition characterized by an excessive accumulation of fat in the liver cells. It is commonly classified into two main types: Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD) and Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (AFLD).

Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)

NAFLD is associated with obesity, diabetes, and metabolic syndrome. It includes a range of liver conditions from simple fatty liver to non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH), which can lead to liver fibrosis and cirrhosis if not managed properly.

Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (AFLD)

AFLD is caused by excessive alcohol consumption and can range from mild fatty liver to severe liver damage including alcoholic hepatitis and cirrhosis. Reducing or eliminating alcohol intake is crucial for managing this condition.

Symptoms and Diagnosis

Fatty liver disease often has no symptoms in its early stages. Symptoms that may appear include fatigue, abdominal discomfort, and unexplained weight loss. Diagnosis typically involves imaging tests, blood tests, and sometimes a liver biopsy to determine the extent of liver damage.
