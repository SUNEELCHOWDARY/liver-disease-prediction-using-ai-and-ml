Cirrhosis Overview
Category Details
Description Cirrhosis is a chronic liver disease characterized by the replacement of healthy liver tissue with scar tissue. This impairs the liver's ability to function properly.
Symptoms Fatigue, weakness, loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, jaundice, abdominal swelling, itchy skin, and easy bruising.
Causes Chronic alcohol consumption, hepatitis B and C infections, nonalcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), autoimmune hepatitis, and genetic disorders.
Prevention Avoiding excessive alcohol intake, maintaining a healthy weight, vaccination against hepatitis B, and managing conditions that affect the liver.
Precautions Regular medical check-ups, avoiding liver-damaging substances, adhering to prescribed treatments, and monitoring liver function.
Treatment Management focuses on addressing the underlying cause, medications to control symptoms, lifestyle changes, and in severe cases, liver transplantation.

Numerous Septa without Cirrhosis
Category Details
Description This condition indicates the presence of numerous septa (fibrous bands) in the liver without the extensive scarring associated with cirrhosis.
Symptoms May not present any specific symptoms; regular monitoring is necessary.
Causes May be associated with liver fibrosis or ongoing liver inflammation.
Prevention Managing underlying liver conditions and avoiding risk factors such as alcohol and certain medications.
Precautions Regular check-ups with a healthcare provider to monitor liver health and prevent progression.
Treatment Focus on addressing the underlying cause and managing liver health through lifestyle changes and medications.
